We've built an internal tool called **Halo Insight** that uses AI to analyze customer feedback across our key touchpoints.

**What it does:**

**Data Sources & Integration**

Halo Insight connects directly to three data sources via their APIs:
- **Survicate** — Analyzes cancellation survey responses to identify churn drivers and trends
- **Gladly** — Reviews support conversations (chat, email, agent notes) to surface common issues and sentiment
- **Zoom** — Zoom API integration is currently in progress and not yet available

While these tools offer external-facing LLM capabilities (like Gladly's customer-facing AI), their employee-facing AI tools are limited. This started when Ken asked, "What are the most common topics in customer support conversations?"—a question that wasn't easily answerable with existing tools.

**Custom Visualizations**

We've built custom charts tailored to our needs—churn trends over time, sentiment analysis, and breakdowns by question and topic. This addresses Survicate's limited visualization capabilities.

**AI-Powered Insights**

The platform uses AI to answer questions like "What specific language do customers use when describing GPS accuracy issues?" or "What themes emerge in cancellation surveys that aren't captured in the predefined questions?" and extracts insights from large volumes of unstructured feedback.

**The Vision**

Halo Insight is an exploration into:
1. **AI-Powered Analysis** — Using AI to extract insights from text at scale
2. **Internal Capability Building** — Developing custom software and AI tools internally

The goal is to have an AI that understands all these data sources holistically—connecting patterns across surveys, support conversations, and chat interactions to provide insights that individual platforms can't offer.
